My First Hackathon: Building 'HawkEye' in 48 Hours

Oct 15, 2025
5 min read

Walking into the venue, the energy was electric. Hundreds of students, endless supplies of caffeine, and a simple goal: build something amazing in 48 hours.

For my first hackathon, I teamed up with three other students to build HawkEye, an object detection web app designed to help visually impaired users identify objects in their surroundings using their phone camera.

The Tech Stack

  • Frontend: HTML/CSS for a responsive mobile interface.
  • Backend: Python (Flask) to handle image processing requests.
  • AI Model: OpenCV and YOLOv3 for real-time object detection.

The Challenge: 3 AM Bug Hunting

Everything was going smoothly until about 3:00 AM on Saturday. We hit a major roadblock trying to stream the video feed from the frontend to the Python backend without significant lag.

After hours of debugging and switching from HTTP requests to WebSockets, we finally got the latency down to under 200ms. The feeling of seeing that first successful detection box draw around a water bottle was incredible.

Lessons Learned

Scope Management

We planned too many features. Cutting 50% of them saved the project.

Git Conflicts

Committing to `main` without pulling first is a recipe for disaster.

HawkEye didn't win first place, but the experience taught me more about software engineering in a weekend than I learned in a month of lectures. I can't wait for the next one.
