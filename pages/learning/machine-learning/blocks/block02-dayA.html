Block 2 • Day A

Supervised Learning Deep Dive

Learning by correction. Every classical ML algorithm builds on the relationship between labeled data and optimization.

1 The Concept of Supervision

Supervised learning is a mathematical mapping: $X \rightarrow y$. The model learns from labeled data—every input (X) is paired with a trusted "Ground Truth" answer (y).

The Student Analogy

"Think of a student solving math problems with an answer key at the back. Every time they get a problem wrong, they adjust their logic until their answers match the key."

2 Regression vs Classification

The type of problem is defined by the nature of the output (y). Predicting a price is fundamentally different from predicting a category.

[Image comparing a regression line plot with a classification decision boundary plot]

Regression

Predicts a continuous number on a line.

  • • House Prices
  • • Temperature
  • • Stock Value

Classification

Predicts a discrete category or class.

  • • Spam / Not Spam
  • • Cat / Dog / Bird
  • • Disease / Healthy

3 The 5 Pillars of Any Supervised System

1

Data (X): Pixel values, word counts, or sensor logs.

2

Labels (y): The ground truth "correct answer."

3

Model: The function that maps $X \rightarrow y$.

4

Loss: The penalty for getting a prediction wrong.

5

Optimization: The method used to change the model and reduce loss.

⚠️ Ground Truth Warning

No algorithm can fix incorrect truth. If your labels are noisy, subjective, or outdated, your model will be fundamentally broken.

"Garbage in, Garbage out."
