Block 6 (2-3 Days)

Regularization & Generalization

Deep networks overfit by default. This block teaches you how to force models to learn the underlying rule, not just the noise.

1 The Overfitting Problem

Overfitting happens when the model is so flexible it "memorizes" the training data, including the noise, causing it to fail on new, unseen data.

Overfitting Signs

Low Training Loss, but High Validation Loss. The model is essentially cheating by looking at the answer key too closely.

Generalization

The true goal of learning. The model captures the underlying pattern (signal), not the specific random fluctuations (noise).

2 Regularization Techniques

Regularization methods intentionally limit the model's freedom to prevent it from fitting the noise. Think of it as adding "constraints" to keep the model honest.

Dropout

Randomly disables neurons during training. This forces the network to be redundant and robust, as it can't rely on any specific neuron to do all the work.

L2 Regularization (Weight Decay)

Penalizes large weights in the loss function. It prevents the model from relying too heavily on any single feature by keeping weight values small and distributed.

$L_{total} = L_{data} + \lambda \sum w^2$

3 The Engineer's Monitoring Habit

You cannot manage what you do not measure. Learning happens in the gap between Training and Validation loss. Use these plots to diagnose your model's health.

Learning Pattern Diagnosis
Both Curves Trending Down Learning well (Healthy)
Train Down, Validation Climbing Up Overfitting (Bad)
Both Curves Flat and High Underfitting (Needs more capacity)

Weight Decay in PyTorch

Implementing weight decay is as simple as passing a parameter to your optimizer constructor.

optimizer = torch.optim.Adam(model.parameters(), weight_decay=1e-4)
