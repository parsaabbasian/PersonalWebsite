Backpropagation

The most important block in Deep Learning. This is how we assign "blame" for an error to millions of weights across the network.

1 The Blame Game

Backpropagation answers one critical question: How should each weight change to reduce the loss? It uses the Chain Rule to flow the error backward from the output layer to the input.

"Forward propagation is for data to make predictions. Backward propagation is for error to find responsibility."

$\frac{dL}{dw} = \frac{dL}{dy} \cdot \frac{dy}{dz} \cdot \frac{dz}{dw}$

The Chain Rule: The heart of every neural network training step.

2 Vanishing & Exploding Gradients

Vanishing

Gradients approach zero. Early layers stop learning. Common with Sigmoid and Tanh because their derivatives are small at extremes.

Exploding

Gradients become excessively large, making training unstable. Common in very deep networks or RNNs.

3 PyTorch's Autograd

You rarely write the chain rule by hand. PyTorch builds a Computation Graph and applies the chain rule automatically when you call loss.backward().

// One line triggers the whole backward flow
# Prediction and loss
output = model(input)
loss = criterion(output, target)

# BACKPROP: Computes dL/dw for every weight in the model
loss.backward()

# Look inside a weight to see its "responsibility"
print(model.layer1.weight.grad)
